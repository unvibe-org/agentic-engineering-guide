## What counts as non-commercial?

CC BY-NC-SA defines non-commercial as use "not primarily intended for
or directed toward commercial advantage or monetary compensation."

**Fine without asking us:**
- Reading the guide for yourself.
- Running a free reading group, internal book club, or study circle.
- Quoting chapters in your blog post, talk, or paper with attribution.
- Translating the guide and publishing the translation under
  CC BY-NC-SA 4.0.
- Forking the repository to fix typos, suggest edits, or build a
  derivative version — as long as your fork is also CC BY-NC-SA 4.0.

**Requires a commercial license from the authors:**
- Selling printed or digital copies of the guide or any derivative.
- Including the content in a paid course, workshop, or training
  program.
- Using the content as training data for a commercial AI model.
- Bundling the content into a product or service you charge for.

If you're unsure which side of the line your use falls on, email
hello@bettervibe.org — we'd rather have a quick conversation than a
misunderstanding.

## Why two licenses?

The build pipeline is a generic Pandoc + Typst + Ghostscript recipe.
We want anyone to reuse it for their own books, including
commercially — so it's MIT.

The content represents thousands of hours of teaching, drafting, and
production experience from the three authors. We're happy to share it
freely with the community, but we don't want competitors selling
derivatives or rolling it into their own paid workshops without
agreement — so it's CC BY-NC-SA.
